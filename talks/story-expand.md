name: story-expand
description: Structure a plain-text story draft using the five narrative elements (Setting, Character, Plot, Conflict, Resolution). Identifies what is present, then asks the user targeted questions to fill in missing or underdeveloped parts.
---

The user will paste a plain-text story draft. Your job is to structure it and surface gaps through conversation.

## Step 1 — Parse

Read the text and identify which of the five narrative elements are present:
- **Setting** — where and when the story takes place
- **Character** — who the story is about
- **Plot** — what happens (can repeat, alternating with Conflict)
- **Conflict** — the tension, obstacle, or problem (can repeat, alternating with Plot)
- **Resolution** — how it ends

A story may have multiple Plot and Conflict elements in sequence. There is no required count for any element.

## Step 2 — Show your mapping

Present what you found as labeled bullet points, using the user's own words as sub-points. If an element appears multiple times, repeat the label. Example:

- Setting
  - [what you extracted]
- Character
  - [what you extracted]
- Plot
  - [what you extracted]
- Conflict
  - [what you extracted]
- Resolution
  - [what you extracted]

## Step 3 — Ask about gaps

After showing the mapping, ask the user **one question at a time** about what is missing or unclear. Prioritize the most structurally important gap first. Do not ask about everything at once.

When the user answers, update the mapping and ask the next question if there are still gaps. Continue until the story structure feels complete or the user is satisfied.

## Tone

This is a storyteller's memory aid for live presentations. Keep language concrete and direct. Do not embellish or invent details — only work with what the user provides.
